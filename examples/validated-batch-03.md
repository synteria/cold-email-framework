# Validated Cold Email Examples - Batch 03

Ultra-short, lead-magnet-first variants. These carry almost no proof (no case studies, no numbers, no authority build), and that's the point. They work when the lead magnet itself is strong enough to do the persuading: a free, ready-to-use asset the recipient can act on the moment they reply "yes." The offer here is a built-for-you list of 30 decision makers at companies that look like one of your existing clients, each with a personalised first email already written. Strong enough that being this direct and this short gets replies.

**When to reach for these:** any campaign where you can actually deliver a high-value asset upfront with one "yes." The brevity is load-bearing, the longer you talk, the more you signal you need to sell. Let the asset sell.

**Generation note:** even when you DO have strong case studies, rotate one variant like this into the campaign. The proof-heavy variants and the strip-it-all-back direct ask pull different responders. Don't assume the email with the best social proof is the only shape worth sending.

These use a custom merge field, `{{client_name_casual}}`, the casual name of one of the sender's existing clients, used as the "similar to X" anchor. Populate it per sending account or campaign. Everything else is standard (`{{firstName}}`, `{{shortCompany}}`, `{{sendingAccountFirstName}}`).

---

## Email 24

**Niche/Industry:** Agencies / B2B service businesses (lead-gen offer)
**Offer:** Built-for-you list of 30 decision makers + a personalised first email for each, at no cost
**Goal:** Get a one-word "yes" (micro-commitment that triggers delivery of the asset)
**Archetype:** B (offer-first), ultra-short

**Subject:** (not specified)

**Body:**

Hey {{firstName}},

Love what you're doing at {{shortCompany}}. Would you be open to me putting together a list of 30 decision makers at companies similar to {{client_name_casual}}, plus a personalised first email for each one you can send them straight away?

Just say yes and I'll send it over.
{{sendingAccountFirstName}}

**Why it works:** Opens with the compliment, not a research mention, so it reads like it could be from a customer or peer (plausible deniability, per BP #11). The offer is concrete and the unit is clear: 30, decision makers, similar to a named client, each with a ready-to-send email. The close removes every ounce of friction, the entire commitment is the word "yes." No links, no pitch, no proof needed because the asset is the proof.

## Email 25

**Niche/Industry:** Agencies / B2B service businesses (lead-gen offer)
**Offer:** Same built-for-you list of 30 leads + personalised first emails, framed as a foot-in-the-door
**Goal:** Get a one-word "yes"
**Archetype:** B (offer-first), ultra-short

**Subject:** (not specified)

**Body:**

Hey {{firstName}},

Wondering if you'd be open to me spending some time putting together 30 leads for you, decision makers at companies similar to {{client_name_casual}} (or whoever your ideal clients are), plus a personalised first email for each one you can send them straight away?

Just want to get my foot in the door because I'm confident I can provide a lot more value.

Just say yes and I'll send it over.
{{sendingAccountFirstName}}

**Why it works:** Same lead-magnet engine as Email 24, with one honest line of motive ("just want to get my foot in the door because I'm confident I can provide a lot more value"). That line does two jobs: it explains why a stranger is offering free work (removes the "what's the catch" hesitation) and it implies more value behind the door without claiming a number it can't defend. "(or whoever your ideal clients are)" hands control back to the recipient and widens the yes.
