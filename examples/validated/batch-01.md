# Validated Cold Email Examples, V2 Batch 01

Status: validated-v2, normalized from the user's revised screenshot HTML.

Numbering restarts here. Old examples are archived as `Legacy Email N`.

Normalization applied:

- `{first name}` and `{{first_name}}` became `{{firstName}}`
- `{Company}`, `{{Company}}`, and `{{company}}` became `{{shortCompany}}`
- hardcoded sender names became `{{sendingAccountFirstName}}`
- money with currency symbols became spoken form, such as `75-90k` and `10M/year`
- default signoffs became `Best,`, `Thanks,`, or a plain close
- flagged source words were omitted from live copy

## Email 1

**Pattern:** same-case opener
**Niche/Industry:** DTC / ecommerce
**Offer:** TikTok Shop revenue growth, introduced in follow-up
**Goal:** get a relevance reply
**Length:** 2 to 4 lines

**Subject:** quick question

**Body:**

Hi {{firstName}},

I talked to a few other 10M/year CMOs, and a lot of them are still trying to crack TikTok Shop.

Same case for {{shortCompany}}?

Best,
{{sendingAccountFirstName}}

PS: saw your LinkedIn post about hiring for CS, how's the search going?

**Why it works, one sentence:** It uses peer context to make the problem feel relevant without hard-diagnosing the prospect.

**Do not overgeneralize:** The first line must be a specific peer pattern, not a generic pain opener.

## Email 2

**Pattern:** reason-I-ask follow-up
**Niche/Industry:** DTC / ecommerce
**Offer:** add 75-90k monthly revenue through TikTok Shop, performance-based
**Goal:** get permission to send a Loom
**Length:** short

**Subject:** {{firstName}}?

**Body:**

Hi {{firstName}},

The reason I ask is I've been helping DTC brands like {{caseStudy}} add another 75-90k in monthly revenue with TikTok Shop, and want to do the same for you, completely on performance.

Can I send a 5-minute Loom explaining how?

Best,
{{sendingAccountFirstName}}

**Why it works, one sentence:** The follow-up reveals the commercial reason only after the relevance check, then asks for a small permission-based yes instead of a call.

**Do not overgeneralize:** Do not include the Loom link in the cold email. Ask permission first.

## Email 3

**Pattern:** social proof follow-up plus value CTA
**Niche/Industry:** agencies / social growth
**Offer:** hands-off Instagram growth with short-form clips
**Goal:** get permission to send a short write-up
**Length:** short

**Subject:** Happy {{dayOfWeek}}, {{firstName}}

**Body:**

Hi {{firstName}},

I've been partnering with agency owners like ABC Media and 123 Marketing to drive hands-off Instagram growth with short-form clips.

Can I send over the 3 things I'd change on your Instagram profile?

Thanks,
{{sendingAccountFirstName}}

**Why it works, one sentence:** One peer proof line earns enough credibility for a concrete permission CTA.

**Do not overgeneralize:** Do not use a format label for the deliverable. Name what the recipient gets.

## Email 4

**Pattern:** personalized first line plus quantified result claim
**Niche/Industry:** agencies / content repurposing
**Offer:** 60 short-form video clips from 30 minutes of the prospect's time
**Goal:** gauge interest in speaking
**Length:** short

**Subject:** Happy {{dayOfWeek}}, {{firstName}}

**Body:**

Hi {{firstName}},

Saw your LinkedIn post about the 123 Marketing Summit, how was it?

If I could make you 60 short-form video clips with 30 minutes of your time, would you be interested in speaking?

Best,
{{sendingAccountFirstName}}

**Why it works, one sentence:** The personal line earns attention, then the offer compresses outcome and effort into one easy-to-answer question.

**Do not overgeneralize:** Only use this shape when the time-input claim is defensible on a call.
